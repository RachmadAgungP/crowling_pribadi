PT Bukit Makmur Mandiri Utama, or often referred to as BUMA, was founded in 1998, and is currently the second largest coal mining contractor in Indonesia operating independently. BUMA was a family business when it was founded, and was later acquired by Delta Dunia in 2009, so that it turned into a professionally operated corporation. BUMA currently has a market share of around 20%, providing mining services to some of the largest and oldest mining companies in the Indonesian coal industry, as well as several new players with high potential for future growth. BUMA carries out comprehensive mining work, starting from overburden removal, coal mining, coal transportation and land reclamation and rehabilitation. BUMA's operations are supported by more than 11,300 employees along with a solid and experienced management team in the field of coal mining, and are facilitated with advanced technology and more than 2,500 units of heavy equipment from various brands such as Komatsu, Caterpillar, Hitachi, Volvo, Scania and Mercedes. Currently, BUMA has two representative offices located in Balikpapan, South Kalimantan and Tanjung Redeb, East Kalimantan.
